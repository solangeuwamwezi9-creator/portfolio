Got Questions?

Everything you need to know before you go

Answers to the most common questions from visitors planning their Musanze adventure.

Categories
General Questions
What is Musanze best known for?+
Musanze (formerly Ruhengeri) is world-famous for gorilla trekking in Volcanoes National Park â€” one of the few places on Earth where mountain gorillas can be seen in their natural habitat. Beyond gorillas, the city is also celebrated for its dramatic volcanic scenery, crater lakes like Ruhondo and Burera, the Musanze Caves, and vibrant Rwandan cultural experiences.
When is the best time to visit Musanze?+
The best times to visit are during Rwanda's two dry seasons: June to September and December to February. The long dry season (Juneâ€“September) offers the most reliable trekking conditions with drier trail surfaces. However, gorilla trekking is available year-round, and many visitors find the green season (Marchâ€“May) beautiful despite the rains â€” the park is lush, misty, and uncrowded.
How many days should I spend in Musanze?+
We recommend a minimum of 3 days: Day 1 for arrival and the Musanze Caves, Day 2 for gorilla trekking, and Day 3 for cultural or lake activities. Five days allows you to experience gorilla trekking, golden monkey trekking, Lake Ruhondo, Iby'Iwacu Cultural Village, and a volcano hike comfortably.
Is Musanze safe for tourists?+
Yes â€” Rwanda is consistently ranked one of Africa's safest travel destinations. Musanze is a welcoming city with low crime rates. All gorilla and golden monkey treks are led by experienced armed park rangers. Solo travelers, families, and senior visitors all explore Musanze safely every day. Standard travel precautions apply, as they would anywhere.
What currency is used and can I use cards?+
Rwanda's currency is the Rwandan Franc (RWF). Most hotels and larger restaurants in Musanze accept Visa and Mastercard. ATMs are available in town. Gorilla permits and park fees can be paid in USD. We recommend carrying some cash in RWF for local markets, moto-taxis, and smaller eateries.
Gorilla Trekking
How do I get a gorilla trekking permit?+
Gorilla trekking permits for Volcanoes National Park cost $1,500 USD per person and must be booked in advance through the Rwanda Development Board (RDB) at rdb.rw or through licensed tour operators. Permits sell out months in advance for peak season â€” we strongly recommend booking at least 3â€“6 months ahead. Only 96 permits are issued daily across 8 gorilla groups.
How physically demanding is gorilla trekking?+
Treks range from 1 to 8 hours depending on where the gorilla family has settled that day. The terrain is steep, dense, and often muddy. You should be in reasonable physical health and comfortable walking at altitude (2,400â€“3,000m). Porters are available at the trailhead for a small fee (~$15) and can carry your pack or even provide physical assistance on steep sections. People aged 15+ can participate.
How long do I spend with the gorillas?+
You spend exactly one hour with the gorilla family, which is strictly enforced to minimize stress on the animals. This limit exists to protect gorilla health and behavioral patterns. Though it sounds short, one hour with a gorilla family â€” watching them groom, play, and go about their day â€” is transformative and deeply emotional.
What should I wear for gorilla trekking?+
Wear long-sleeved shirts and long trousers (even on warm days â€” to protect against nettles and insects), sturdy waterproof hiking boots with ankle support, a rain jacket, and gardening gloves for grabbing vegetation. Pack light: a small daypack with water, snacks, a camera, and sunscreen. Avoid bright colors and perfume near the gorillas.
Can I photograph the gorillas?+
Yes, photography is permitted and encouraged! Keep your camera on silent mode (no flash), and maintain the required 7-meter distance. Experienced photographers love early morning treks when soft mist light filters through the forest canopy. Video is also allowed. Tripods are generally impractical on the trails.
Getting There & Around
How do I get from Kigali to Musanze?+
The most popular options are: (1) Private car or taxi â€” approximately 2â€“2.5 hours via the well-paved RN4 highway, costing $60â€“100 for a private transfer. (2) Public bus from Nyabugogo Bus Terminal in Kigali â€” reliable, frequent, and costs around RWF 2,500 (~$2.50). Journey takes about 2.5â€“3 hours. (3) Tour operator vehicle â€” recommended if you have a gorilla permit, as operators can arrange door-to-door service.
How do I get around in Musanze?+
The most popular and affordable local transport is moto-taxi (motorcycle taxi), which costs RWF 500â€“2,000 for rides around town and to nearby attractions. Car hire with driver is available for full-day excursions to the lakes and national park. Most lodges can arrange airport pickups and guided day trips. Walking is pleasant for exploring Musanze town itself.
Is there accommodation in Musanze to suit all budgets?+
Absolutely. Musanze offers a wide range of accommodation: budget guesthouses from $20â€“40/night, comfortable mid-range hotels at $60â€“120/night, and world-class luxury eco-lodges (such as Bisate Lodge and One&Only Gorilla's Nest) from $300â€“700+/night. We recommend booking well in advance during peak season (Juneâ€“September), especially for luxury properties.
Is there internet and mobile connectivity?+
MTN Rwanda and Airtel Rwanda provide good 4G coverage in Musanze town and most lodges. Signal is weak or absent deep inside the national park. Most hotels and restaurants offer WiFi. Consider purchasing a local SIM card at Kigali airport (available from $2â€“5 with data packages) for reliable and affordable connectivity during your trip.
Safety & Health
Do I need vaccinations to visit Rwanda?+
Yellow Fever vaccination is mandatory for entry into Rwanda and you must carry proof of vaccination. Routine vaccinations (tetanus, hepatitis A/B, typhoid) are recommended. Malaria prophylaxis is advised â€” Musanze is at altitude and lower risk than other areas, but precautions are still worthwhile. Consult a travel health clinic 4â€“6 weeks before departure.
Do I need travel insurance?+
Yes, comprehensive travel insurance is strongly recommended and required by most tour operators. Ensure your policy covers emergency medical evacuation, trip cancellation (gorilla permits are typically non-refundable), and adventure activities including hiking. Medical facilities in Musanze are limited for serious emergencies â€” evacuation to Kigali may be required.
Are there any rules when near gorillas?+
Yes, strict rules protect both gorillas and visitors: maintain at least 7 meters distance at all times, no flash photography, do not eat or drink in the gorilla presence, wear a surgical mask if you have any illness (rangers provide these), do not make sudden movements or loud noises, follow ranger instructions at all times, and never block the gorillas' path. These rules exist to protect animals that share 98.3% of human DNA.
Culture & Etiquette
What are some important cultural customs to know?+
Rwandans are warm and hospitable. Greet elders respectfully â€” a handshake or gentle bow is appropriate. Remove shoes before entering someone's home. Plastic bags are banned in Rwanda (enforce this strictly at the airport too). Dress modestly outside resort areas â€” avoid very short shorts or revealing clothing. Tipping is appreciated but not always expected; 10% is a kind gesture at restaurants.
What language is spoken in Musanze?+
Kinyarwanda is the official local language. English is widely spoken in tourism, hotels, and national parks. French is also common among older generations. Learning a few Kinyarwanda phrases goes a long way â€” "Muraho" (hello), "Murakoze" (thank you), and "Amakuru?" (how are you?) are always warmly received by locals.
What souvenirs should I look for?+
Rwanda is famous for its traditional Agaseke peace baskets â€” intricate woven baskets with geometric patterns, produced by women's cooperatives. Other popular souvenirs include imigongo (geometric cow dung art), wooden carvings, locally produced coffee and tea, and hand-painted fabrics. The Musanze town market and Iby'Iwacu cultural village are excellent places to buy authentic, fairly-traded goods.
What Rwandan food should I try in Musanze?+
Don't miss brochettes (grilled meat skewers), ubugali (cassava or maize dough), isombe (cassava leaves with groundnut sauce), and imineke (grilled plantains). For breakfast, try mandazi (fried dough) with Rwanda's incredible locally grown coffee. The Chez Lando and Inzu Lodge restaurants in Musanze serve excellent traditional Rwandan set menus at very reasonable prices.
